House Key

The teeth are new, but the bow is polished smooth where a thumb has worried it for months. The leather tag is cracked, showing older letters beneath the top layer.

On the edge: a faint, greasy residue. Not hers—she keeps things too clean.

When the key turns in your hand, it rattles. Something inside the tag. A tiny folded paper, halves stuck together.
